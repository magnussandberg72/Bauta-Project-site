🌍 BAUTA Projects – Angola Edition

Free of Charge – For Africa

These twenty large-scale humanitarian projects form the foundation of BAUTA – Vision Framtid’s mission to bring free healthcare, food, energy and water to the entire African continent. Each project operates with love, faith, and technology, serving humanity without cost or condition.

🌾 Free Food Network

Hydrogen-powered logistics delivering free meals across Africa.

🧊 Cold Storage Facilities

Solar-cooled hubs keep distributed food fresh and safe.

🌊 Wave Energy Stations

20 coastal machines converting ocean power into clean hydrogen.

💨 Offshore Wind Parks

50 turbines generating renewable energy for continental supply.

☀️ Solar Parks

20 solar plants turning sunlight into hydrogen & stable power grids.

💊 Natural Medicine Factories

Free natural therapeutics for hospitals and clinics.

🏥 Free Hospitals Network

30 modern hospitals offering completely free healthcare.

🧪 Pharmaceutical Factories

Produces insulin, antibiotics and vaccines free of charge.

💧 Water Extraction & Purification

Solar-powered wells bring clean drinking water to millions.

💰 Microloan Bank Network

Five microloan banks empowering entrepreneurs and families.

🚛 Hydrogen ICE Fleet

1 000 green transport vehicles for logistics and aid delivery.

🧠 AI Medical Research Center

AI-driven precision medicine for Africa’s diseases.

📱 Digital Health & Telemedicine

Free remote consultations connecting rural areas and doctors.

👶 Maternal & Child Clinics

Providing prenatal and pediatric care – reducing infant mortality.

🌿 Food Security & Agro-Tech

AI-driven irrigation and smart farming for self-sufficiency.

⚗️ Water-to-Hydrogen Labs

Electrolysis facilities producing local green fuel.

🎓 STEM Education Centers

Training youth in AI, energy and medicine fields.

🛣️ Continental Logistics Corridors

Hydrogen routes linking regions and distribution hubs.

🚜 24/7 Water Drilling Fleet

1 000 rigs drilling wells day and night – one in every village.

🌐 Internet & Data Connectivity

Solar satellite hubs bringing 15 million Africans online.
